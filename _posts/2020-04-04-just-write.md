# Write!

Incredible. It seems so easy. Just write. I'm guessing, hoping I'm not the only person that struggles with this.

I'm working on a few things, reading, and listening to podcasts like always. A theme has been popping up constantly. Just write!

I have been reading a lot of [Seth Godin's blog](https://seths.blog/) lately and listening to his two-minute audio blips in [Blinkist](https://blinkist.com). He writes, actually, he produces all kinds of content every single day. He suggests it doesn't matter if it is good or not. Just write. Eventually, something good will come out. He also mentions it doesn't really matter, just write. Produce content. Every day.

I have committed a few times to trying. But, here we go again. You never fail unless you give up, right!?

One of the main issues I bump into is not simply writing for myself, but feeling like I have to write for you, the "audience". Which, ironically, is non-existent, so not sure why I am so worried about "you".

From now on, I'm picking things out of my [Zettelkasten](https://zettelkasten.de/) (more on this later) to write about. They may be on topic for security, or on a complete different topic. I'm simply giving myself freedom to write.

I'm not going to concern myself with content length, perfect images, staying on topic, or other reasons to avoid writing, I'm simply going to write. Hopefully, it isn't overly painful to read. If it is, well, hopefully it improves quickly.

I am going to _just write. Every. Single. Day._
